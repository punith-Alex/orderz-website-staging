The gaps in a cafe operation show up most when the queue is longest.

Order customisation errors that reach the customer

Extra shot. Oat milk. No sugar. Half sweet. Cafes handle more order variations per drink than almost any other F&B category. When customisations are taken verbally and written by hand, errors are not occasional — they are inevitable. The customer who got full sugar when they asked for none does not come back.

Inventory running out mid-shift with no early warning

You run out of oat milk at 10am on a Saturday. You do not know until your barista cannot complete the order already rung up at the counter. You either apologise to the customer or send someone to buy from the nearest supermarket at retail price. Neither is acceptable.

Morning rush slows to a crawl at the counter

The queue at 8:30am is the most important 30 minutes of your day. If customers are waiting more than 3 minutes, they leave — to the cafe across the street with a mobile order app. The bottleneck is not your baristas. It is the order-taking and billing process at the counter.

Regulars who feel like strangers every visit

Your best customers come in three times a week. They order the same thing every time. But because there is no customer record, your staff asks them the same questions every visit. No loyalty recognition. No personalisation. No reason to choose your cafe over the next one.

No clear picture of which drinks are driving revenue

Matcha latte is your most ordered drink but your lowest margin item. Your single-origin pour-over sells half as many but drives more profit per cup. Without a daily sales breakdown by item, you are pricing and promoting by gut feel instead of data.

OrderZ connects your order flow, inventory, customer records, and reporting — so your cafe runs on data, not guesswork.
